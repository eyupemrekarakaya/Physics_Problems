    1. Projectile Motion

    A projectile is launched from the ground with initial speed $\(v_0 = 100\ \text{m/s}\)$ at an angle of $\(\theta = 37^\circ\)$ above the horizontal. Assume no air resistance.

    (a) Differential equations of motion

    Since there is no air resistance, the only force acting on the projectile is gravity.

    In the horizontal direction:

    \[ m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = 0 \]

    So,

    \[ \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = 0 \]

    In the vertical direction:

    \[ m\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -mg \]

    So,

    \[ \frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -g \]

    with

    \[ g = 9.8\ \text{m/s}^2 \]

    The initial velocity components are:

    \[ v_{0x} = v_0\cos\theta = 100\cos 37^\circ \]
    \[ v_{0y} = v_0\sin\theta = 100\sin 37^\circ \]

    Using approximate values:

    \[ \cos 37^\circ \approx 0.7986,\qquad \sin 37^\circ \approx 0.6018 \]

    therefore,

    \[ v_{0x} \approx 79.86\ \text{m/s} \]
    \[ v_{0y} \approx 60.18\ \text{m/s} \]

    (b) Time of flight

    For a projectile launched and landing at the same height, the time of flight is

    \[ T = \frac{2v_{0y}}{g} \]

    Substituting the values:

    \[ T = \frac{2(60.18)}{9.8} \]
    \[ T \approx 12.28\ \text{s} \]

    (c) Maximum height

    At the maximum height, the vertical velocity becomes zero. Using

    \[ H = \frac{v_{0y}^2}{2g} \]

    we get

    \[ H = \frac{(60.18)^2}{2(9.8)} \]
    \[ H \approx \frac{3621.63}{19.6} \]
    \[ H \approx 184.78\ \text{m} \]

    (d) Range

    The horizontal range is

    \[ R = v_{0x} \cdot T \]

    Substituting:

    \[ R = 79.86 \times 12.28 \]
    \[ R \approx 980.68\ \text{m} \]

    Alternatively, using the standard formula:

    \[ R = \frac{v_0^2\sin 2\theta}{g} \]
    \[ R = \frac{100^2\sin 74^\circ}{9.8} \]

    which gives the same result approximately:

    \[ R \approx 980.7\ \text{m} \]

    Final Answers

    \[ \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = 0 \]
    \[ \frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -g \]
    \[ T \approx 12.28\ \text{s} \]
    \[ H_{\max} \approx 184.78\ \text{m} \]
    \[ R \approx 980.68\ \text{m} \]
