## 5. Inelastic Collision

Since the runner jumps onto the cart and they move together, this is a **perfectly inelastic collision**, so momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Given:

$$
m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}, \quad v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}
$$

$$
m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}, \quad v_2 = 0
$$

Substitute:

$$
(70)(3) + (140)(0) = (210)v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

**Answer:** The final speed of the cart with the runner is

$$
v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Is kinetic energy conserved?

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
KE_i = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2
$$

$$
KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2) = 315 \text{ J}
$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$
KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1 + m_2)v_f^2
$$

$$
KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2) = 105 \text{ J}
$$

Since:

$$
KE_f < KE_i
$$

**Answer:** Kinetic energy is **not conserved**. Some energy is lost (e.g., as heat, sound, and deformation).
