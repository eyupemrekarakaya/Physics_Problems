## 3. Conservation of Energy

The speed of a pendulum at the bottom can be found using conservation of energy:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

The vertical height difference is:

$$
h = L(1 - \cos\theta)
$$

Given:

$$
L = 1.0 \text{ m}, \quad \theta = 15^\circ
$$

So,

$$
h = 1(1 - \cos 15^\circ)
$$

$$
\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.966
$$

$$
h \approx 1(1 - 0.966) = 0.034 \text{ m}
$$

Using energy conservation:

$$
mg h = \frac{1}{2} m v^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
g h = \frac{1}{2} v^2
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ and $h = 0.034$:

$$
v = \sqrt{2(9.8)(0.034)}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{0.6664}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.82 \text{ m/s}
$$

**Answer:** The speed at the bottom is approximately

$$
v \approx 0.82 \text{ m/s}
$$
