## 4. Energy & Momentum

First, find the speed of the 0.5 kg block at the bottom using conservation of energy:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute $g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ and $h = 3.0 \text{ m}$:

$$
v = \sqrt{2(9.8)(3.0)}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{58.8}
$$

$$
v \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Collision (Conservation of Momentum)

Since the blocks stick together, this is a perfectly inelastic collision:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Given:

$$
m_1 = 0.5, \quad v_1 = 7.67, \quad m_2 = 1.5, \quad v_2 = 0
$$

$$
(0.5)(7.67) + (1.5)(0) = (2.0)v_f
$$

$$
3.835 = 2.0 v_f
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

**Answer:** The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$
