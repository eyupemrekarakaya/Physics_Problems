## 7. Dynamics with Friction

We analyze the forces acting on the **10 kg block**.

Since the **5 kg block is tied to the wall**, the 10 kg block slides beneath it. Therefore, there are two kinetic friction forces opposing the motion of the 10 kg block:

1. Friction between the **10 kg block and the ground**
2. Friction between the **5 kg block and the 10 kg block**

---

### Step 1: Friction between the 10 kg block and the ground

The ground supports both blocks, so the normal force is:

$$
N_{\text{ground}} = (10 + 5)g = 15g
$$

Thus, the kinetic friction from the ground is:

$$
f_{\text{ground}} = \mu_k N_{\text{ground}}
$$

$$
f_{\text{ground}} = 0.2 \times 15 \times 9.8
$$

$$
f_{\text{ground}} = 29.4 \text{ N}
$$

---

### Step 2: Friction between the 5 kg block and the 10 kg block

The normal force between the two blocks is due to the weight of the 5 kg block:

$$
N_{\text{top}} = 5g
$$

So the kinetic friction between them is:

$$
f_{\text{top}} = \mu_k N_{\text{top}}
$$

$$
f_{\text{top}} = 0.2 \times 5 \times 9.8
$$

$$
f_{\text{top}} = 9.8 \text{ N}
$$

This friction also opposes the motion of the 10 kg block.

---

### Step 3: Net force on the 10 kg block

Applied force:

$$
F = 45 \text{ N}
$$

Total opposing friction:

$$
f_{\text{total}} = f_{\text{ground}} + f_{\text{top}}
$$

$$
f_{\text{total}} = 29.4 + 9.8 = 39.2 \text{ N}
$$

Net force:

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 45 - 39.2
$$

$$
F_{\text{net}} = 5.8 \text{ N}
$$

---

### Step 4: Acceleration of the 10 kg block

Using Newton's second law:

$$
F_{\text{net}} = ma
$$

$$
a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m}
$$

$$
a = \frac{5.8}{10}
$$

$$
a = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

**Answer:** The acceleration of the **10 kg block** is

$$
a = 0.58 \text{ m/s}^2
$$
