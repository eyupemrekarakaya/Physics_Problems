## 6. Energy Dissipation

The mechanical energy of the ball is proportional to its height:

$$
E \propto h
$$

After each bounce, the ball retains 70% of its energy (since it loses 30%):

$$
E_{\text{after}} = 0.7 E_{\text{before}}
$$

Thus, the height also scales by 0.7 after each bounce.

Initial height:

$$
h_0 = 2.0 \text{ m}
$$

---

### After first bounce

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \times 2.0 = 1.4 \text{ m}
$$

---

### After second bounce

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \times 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98 \text{ m}
$$

---

**Answer:** The ball rises to approximately

$$
h_2 \approx 0.98 \text{ m}
$$
