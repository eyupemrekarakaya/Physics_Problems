## 8. Work of a Variable Force

Given the force:

$$
F(x) = -kx
$$

---

### Equation of Motion

Using Newton’s second law:

$$
F = ma
$$

$$
m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -kx
$$

Rewriting:

$$
\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \frac{k}{m}x = 0
$$

This is the equation of **simple harmonic motion**.

General solution:

$$
x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)
$$

where

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

---

### Work Done from $0$ to $x_0$

Work is given by:

$$
W = \int_0^{x_0} F(x)\,dx
$$

$$
W = \int_0^{x_0} (-kx)\,dx
$$

$$
W = -k \int_0^{x_0} x\,dx
$$

$$
W = -k \left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_0^{x_0}
$$

$$
W = -\frac{1}{2}k x_0^2
$$

---

### Interpretation as Potential Energy

Since work done by the force is related to potential energy:

$$
W = -\Delta U
$$

So,

$$
U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
$$

---

### Verification of $F = -\frac{dU}{dx}$

$$
\frac{dU}{dx} = kx
$$

$$
F(x) = -\frac{dU}{dx} = -kx
$$

✔️ Verified

---

### Graphs

- $F(x) = -kx$ → straight line through origin with negative slope  
- $U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$ → upward-opening parabola  

**Answer:**  
- Equation of motion: simple harmonic motion  
- Work:  

$$
W = -\frac{1}{2}k x_0^2
$$  

- Potential energy:  

$$
U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2
$$
