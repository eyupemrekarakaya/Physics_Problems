## 2. Harmonic Motion

A mass-spring system in simple harmonic motion can be written as:

$$
x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)
$$

From the given equation:

$$
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
$$

we identify:

$$
A = 0.2 \text{ m}
$$

and

$$
\omega = 10\pi \text{ rad/s}
$$

For a mass-spring system, the angular frequency is related to the spring constant by:

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

So,

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

Substituting $m = 10 \text{ kg}$ and $\omega = 10\pi$:

$$
k = 10(10\pi)^2
$$

$$
k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2
$$

$$
k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}
$$

$$
k \approx 1000(9.87) = 9870 \text{ N/m}
$$

**Answer:** The spring constant is

$$
k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m} \approx 9870 \text{ N/m}
$$

### Total Mechanical Energy

The total mechanical energy of a spring-mass system is:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

Substitute $k = 1000\pi^2$ and $A = 0.2$:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.2)^2
$$

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.04)
$$

$$
E = 20\pi^2 \text{ J}
$$

$$
E \approx 20(9.87) = 197.4 \text{ J}
$$

**Answer:** The total mechanical energy is

$$
E = 20\pi^2 \text{ J} \approx 197.4 \text{ J}
$$
