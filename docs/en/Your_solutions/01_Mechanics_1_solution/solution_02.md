## 2. Range Optimization

For projectile motion, the horizontal range is given by

$$
R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}
$$

### Maximizing the range

To find the angle that maximizes the range, we take the derivative of $$R(\theta)$$ with respect to $$\theta$$:

$$
\frac{dR}{d\theta} = \frac{v_0^2}{g} \cdot \frac{d}{d\theta}[\sin(2\theta)]
$$

Using the chain rule:

$$
\frac{d}{d\theta}[\sin(2\theta)] = 2\cos(2\theta)
$$

So,

$$
\frac{dR}{d\theta} = \frac{2v_0^2}{g}\cos(2\theta)
$$

### Critical point

Set the derivative equal to zero:

$$
\frac{2v_0^2}{g}\cos(2\theta) = 0
$$

$$
\cos(2\theta) = 0
$$

This occurs when:

$$
2\theta = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

### Maximum check

Since $$\sin(2\theta)$$ reaches its maximum value of 1 when $$2\theta = 90^\circ$$, the range is maximized at:

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

### Maximum range

Substitute $$\sin(2\theta) = 1$$ into the range equation:

$$
R_{\max} = \frac{v_0^2}{g}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}
$$

$$
\theta_{\text{max}} = 45^\circ
$$

$$
R_{\max} = \frac{v_0^2}{g}
$$
