## 8. Circular Motion

A person standing on the Earth's equator moves in a circle due to the Earth's rotation.

The centripetal acceleration is given by

$$
a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}
$$

or equivalently,

$$
a_c = \omega^2 r
$$

where

- $$r = 6378\ \text{km} = 6.378\times 10^6\ \text{m}$$
- the angular speed is

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

Assuming one full rotation takes

$$
T = 24 \times 60 \times 60 = 86400\ \text{s}
$$

---

### Step 1: Calculate angular speed

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{86400}
$$

$$
\omega \approx 7.27\times 10^{-5}\ \text{rad/s}
$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate centripetal acceleration

Using

$$
a_c = \omega^2 r
$$

we get

$$
a_c = (7.27\times 10^{-5})^2(6.378\times 10^6)
$$

$$
a_c \approx 0.0337\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
a_c = \omega^2 r
$$

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.27\times 10^{-5}\ \text{rad/s}
$$

$$
a_c \approx 3.37\times 10^{-2}\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

So, the centripetal acceleration at the Earth's equator is

$$
\boxed{a_c \approx 0.034\ \text{m/s}^2}
$$
