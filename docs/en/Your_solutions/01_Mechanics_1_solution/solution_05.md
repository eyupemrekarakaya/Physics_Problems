## 5. Relative Velocity

A river flows east with speed

$$
v_r = 2\ \text{m/s}
$$

and the boat's speed in still water is

$$
v_b = 5\ \text{m/s}
$$

The boat wants to go **directly north**, so its westward component must cancel the river's eastward flow.

---

### Step 1: Resolve the boat's velocity

Let $$\theta$$ be the angle **west of north** that the boat must head.

The westward component of the boat's velocity must equal the river speed:

$$
5\sin\theta = 2
$$

So,

$$
\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)
$$

$$
\theta \approx 23.58^\circ
$$

So the boat should head

$$
23.58^\circ \text{ west of north}
$$

---

### Step 2: Find the effective northward speed

The northward component of the boat's velocity is

$$
v_{\text{north}} = 5\cos\theta
$$

Using

$$
\cos\theta = \sqrt{1-\sin^2\theta}
= \sqrt{1-\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2}
= \sqrt{\frac{21}{25}}
= \frac{\sqrt{21}}{5}
$$

we get

$$
v_{\text{north}} = 5\cdot \frac{\sqrt{21}}{5} = \sqrt{21}
$$

$$
v_{\text{north}} \approx 4.58\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

### Step 3: Time to cross the river

The river is $$200\ \text{m}$$ wide, so the crossing time is

$$
t = \frac{200}{v_{\text{north}}}
$$

$$
t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.64\ \text{s}
$$

---

## Final Result

$$
\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{2}{5}\right) \approx 23.58^\circ
$$

$$
\text{Direction: } 23.58^\circ \text{ west of north}
$$

$$
v_{\text{north}} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58\ \text{m/s}
$$

$$
t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx 43.64\ \text{s}
$$
