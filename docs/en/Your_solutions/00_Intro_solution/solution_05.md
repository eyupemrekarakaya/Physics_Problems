# Explanation — Problem 05 (Trigonometry)

## Problem

A vector **A** has a magnitude of 15 and makes an angle of **60° with the horizontal axis**.  
Calculate its **horizontal and vertical components**.

---

## Idea of Vector Components

Any vector in two dimensions can be decomposed into two perpendicular components:

- a **horizontal component** along the x-axis
- a **vertical component** along the y-axis

These components form a **right triangle**, where the vector itself is the **hypotenuse**.

---

## Using Trigonometry

From trigonometry:

- cosine relates the **adjacent side** to the hypotenuse
- sine relates the **opposite side** to the hypotenuse

Therefore:

Horizontal component:

$$ 
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

Vertical component:

\[
A_y = A \sin\theta
\]

---

## Substitute the Values

Vector magnitude:

\[
A = 15
\]

Angle:

\[
\theta = 60^\circ
\]

### Horizontal component

\[
A_x = 15\cos60^\circ
\]

Since

\[
\cos60^\circ = 0.5
\]

\[
A_x = 15 \times 0.5
\]

\[
A_x = 7.5
\]

---

### Vertical component

\[
A_y = 15\sin60^\circ
\]

We know:

\[
\sin60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt3}{2}
\]

So:

\[
A_y = 15\frac{\sqrt3}{2}
\]

Numerically:

\[
A_y \approx 12.99
\]

---

## Final Result

The components of the vector are:

\[
A_x = 7.5
\]

\[
A_y = 15\frac{\sqrt3}{2} \approx 12.99
\]

So the vector can be written as:

\[
\vec A = (7.5,\; 12.99)
\]

---

## Interpretation

This means the vector moves:

- **7.5 units horizontally**
- **about 13 units vertically**
