# Solution 03 — Proportionality

## Problem

Consider the Universal Law of Gravitation:

F = G * (m1 * m2) / r^2

Determine the factor by which the force changes if:
- the distance r is doubled
- both masses m1 and m2 are halved.

---

## Solution

Original force:

F = G * (m1 * m2) / r^2

### Step 1 — Modify masses

m1' = m1 / 2  
m2' = m2 / 2  

m1' * m2' = (m1 * m2) / 4

### Step 2 — Modify distance

r' = 2r

r'^2 = (2r)^2 = 4r^2

### Step 3 — New force

F' = G * (m1' * m2') / r'^2

Substitute:

F' = G * ((m1*m2)/4) / (4r^2)

F' = G * (m1*m2) / (16r^2)

---

## Answer

F' = F / 16

The gravitational force becomes **16 times smaller**.
