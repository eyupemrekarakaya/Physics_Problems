## 1. Wave Properties

A sound wave in air has a frequency of 440 Hz. If the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s, what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is 1482 m/s?

### Given:

- Frequency: $$f = 440 \,\text{Hz}$$  
- Speed in air: $$v_{\text{air}} = 343 \,\text{m/s}$$  
- Speed in water: $$v_{\text{water}} = 1482 \,\text{m/s}$$  

---

### Formula

$$
\lambda = \frac{v}{f}
$$

---

### Wavelength in Air

$$
\lambda_{\text{air}} = \frac{343}{440} \approx 0.78 \,\text{m}
$$

---

### Wavelength in Water

$$
\lambda_{\text{water}} = \frac{1482}{440} \approx 3.37 \,\text{m}
$$

---

### Final Answers

- Air: $$0.78 \,\text{m}$$  
- Water: $$3.37 \,\text{m}$$

## 2. String Harmonics

A guitar string is 64 cm long and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of 330 Hz. What is the speed of the wave on this string?

### Given:

- Length: $$L = 0.64 \,\text{m}$$  
- Frequency: $$f = 330 \,\text{Hz}$$  

---

### Fundamental Relation

$$
\lambda = 2L
$$

---

### Wavelength

$$
\lambda = 2 \times 0.64 = 1.28 \,\text{m}
$$

---

### Wave Speed Formula

$$
v = f\lambda
$$

---

### Calculation

$$
v = 330 \times 1.28 = 422.4 \,\text{m/s}
$$

---

### Final Answer

$$
\boxed{v \approx 422.4 \,\text{m/s}}
$$
