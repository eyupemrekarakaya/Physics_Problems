## 3. Superposition Principle

Two waves are described by:

$$
y_1(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t), \quad y_2(x,t) = A \sin(kx + \omega t)
$$

---

### Superposition

$$
y(x,t) = y_1 + y_2
$$

Using identity:

$$
\sin(a - b) + \sin(a + b) = 2 \sin(a)\cos(b)
$$

---

### Resulting Standing Wave

$$
y(x,t) = 2A \sin(kx)\cos(\omega t)
$$

---

### Nodes

$$
\sin(kx) = 0
$$

$$
kx = n\pi
$$

---

### Node Positions

$$
x = \frac{n\pi}{k}
$$

---

### Final Answers

- Standing wave:
$$
\boxed{y(x,t) = 2A \sin(kx)\cos(\omega t)}
$$

- Node positions:
$$
\boxed{x = \frac{n\pi}{k}}
$$
