## 8. Waves

Which of the following functions can describe a traveling wave?

---

### Condition

A traveling wave must have the form:

$$
y(x,t) = f(x \pm vt)
$$

---

### a)

$$
y(x,t) = A\cos(kx^2 - \omega t)
$$

Not valid because the argument contains $x^2$ (not linear in $x$).

---

### b)

$$
y(x,t) = A(x - vt)^2
$$

Valid traveling wave since it is of the form $f(x - vt)$.

---

### c)

$$
y(x,t) = A\log(x + vt)
$$

Valid traveling wave since it is of the form $f(x + vt)$.

---

### Final Answer

$$
\boxed{b \text{ and } c}
$$
